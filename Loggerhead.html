The Loggerhead Sea Turtle

Caretta caretta

An Alabama Resident on the Endangered Species List

About the Loggerhead üê¢

  • Name Origin: Named for its massive head and powerful jaws, used to crush shells.
  • Size: Adults weigh 155 to 375 pounds (70-170 kg).
  • Length: About 3 feet (90 cm) in carapace (shell) length.
  • Lifespan: Estimated to be 70-80+ years.
  • Diet: Carnivores, feeding on bottom-dwelling invertebrates like conchs, crabs, and clams.
  • Conservation Status: Listed as Threatened under the U.S. Endangered Species Act.

Habitat and Range

Loggerheads are found in temperate and tropical regions of the Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans. They are highly migratory, undertaking long journeys between feeding grounds and nesting beaches.

Loggerheads in Alabama

In Alabama, Loggerheads are found in the coastal waters of the Gulf of Mexico.

  • Key Location: Nesting primarily occurs on the beaches of Baldwin County and the Fort Morgan Peninsula.
  • Important Nesting Area: The Bon Secour National Wildlife Refuge is a critical, protected nesting site.
  • Nesting Season: Typically runs from May through August.

Nesting Habits ü•ö

Females return to the same general areas where they were born to lay their eggs.

  • At night, a female digs a chamber and lays a clutch of about 100-120 eggs.
  • The eggs incubate for about 60 days. Sand temperature determines the sex of the hatchlings!
  • Hatchlings emerge together at night and rush towards the ocean, guided by moonlight on the water.

How They Move

In the Water

They are powerful and graceful swimmers. Their long front flippers provide propulsion in a figure-8 motion, like a bird's wings. Rear flippers act as rudders for steering.

On Land

On the beach, a female Loggerhead moves by pulling herself forward with her front flippers in an alternating (left-right) gait. This is a key identifier for their tracks!

Data for Your AI Tracking Model

Identifying a turtle's crawl (tracks) is the first step in protecting a nest. Your AI model can learn to identify the unique crawl of a Loggerhead.

Key Identifiers:

  • Asymmetrical Marks: The left and right flipper marks are not parallel due to the alternating gait.
  • No "Body Pit": Unlike Green turtles, they don't dig a large body pit.
  • Tail Drag Mark: A straight drag mark from their tail is often visible down the center.
  • Track Width: Typically 30-40 inches for an adult.

Note the alternating, comma-shaped flipper marks.
